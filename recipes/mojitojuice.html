Mojito cocktail Juice



Description

The Mojito is a classic cocktail known for its refreshing and vibrant flavors.
It is a popular drink originating from Cuba and has gained worldwide popularity for
its combination of mint, lime, rum, and soda water. The Mojito is often enjoyed on warm
summer days or as a tropical escape in any season.
The drink starts with the base of white rum, which provides a smooth and slightly sweet alcoholic kick.
Freshly squeezed lime juice adds a zesty and tangy flavor that balances the sweetness of the other ingredients.
Granulated sugar is used to enhance the sweetness, and it is muddled together with fresh mint leaves to release
their aromatic oils and create a refreshing herbal essence.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz (60 ml) white rum
  • 1 oz (30 ml) freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar (adjust according to taste)
  • 6-8 fresh mint leaves
  • Soda water
  • Crushed ice
  • Lime wedges and mint sprigs for garnish

Instructions to be followed in making Mojito Drinks juice

  1. In a sturdy glass or cocktail shaker, add the fresh mint leaves and granulated sugar.
  2. Gently muddle the mint leaves and sugar together to release the oils and flavors from the mint.
  3. Add the freshly squeezed lime juice to the glass or shaker and continue to muddle briefly to combine the ingredients.
  4. Fill the glass or shaker with crushed ice.
  5. Pour in the white rum over the ice.
  6. Stir the mixture well to ensure the flavors are evenly distributed.
  7. Fill a highball glass with fresh ice cubes.
  8. Strain the mixture from the shaker into the glass, or pour directly from the glass if you muddled the ingredients in the glass itself.
  9. Top off the glass with soda water, leaving some space at the top for garnish and stirring.
  10. Gently stir the drink to combine all the ingredients.
  11. Garnish the Mojito with a lime wedge and a sprig of fresh mint.
  12. Serve and enjoy your refreshing Mojito!
